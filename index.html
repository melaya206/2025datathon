Department Service Requests by Year

This 3D line plot illustrates the volume of customer service requests handled by each Seattle city department annually from 2021 to early 2025. Each department is represented by a distinct colored line, allowing for clear comparison of service demand patterns over time. The x-axis displays the different city departments, while the y-axis shows the progression of years. The z-axis represents the total number of customer service requests received during each time period, with higher values indicating greater service demand. This interactive visualization enables stakeholders to identify trends, compare departmental workloads, and analyze how service request volumes have evolved across different departments during this four-year period. Clicking on a department in the key will toggle the visibility of that department's line.

Service Request Distribution

This visualization shows total number of customer service requests submitted to the City of Seattle, categorized by service type, from January 2021 through early April 2025, with rankings for each month. Each service type is represented by unique trend bar, allowing easy comparison over time. On the y-axis are rankings of the most requested service during the selected month with rank #1 being the most requested service. The x-axis shows a running sum of the total number of requests sent overall. This visualization is used to inform trends for each service type.

Notable Trends

Unauthorized Encampment was first reported in July 2022 with 4,343 requests, but grew to be the second most requested service by April 2025 at 129,102 request -- almost 30 times larger. Parking Enforcement remained a frequent service request throughout the past four years. Illegal parking parking has slight increases during summer months, suggesting a potential seasonal pattern linked to higher city activity. Abandoned Vehicle/72hr Parking Ordinance became more frequently requested later on but eventually overcame all requests by far as the most prominent service type. A clear upward trend begins in early 2023 and steadily rises into 2024. This suggests that abandoned vehicles have become an increasingly significant concern for residents in recent years. Graffiti Removal requests show significant growth over time, especially starting mid-2022. Overall Insights

Several service types exhibit seasonal variation, hinting at environmental or behavioral influences on request volumes. Unauthorized Encampment, Abandoned Vehicle, and Graffiti Removal requests show the most significant growth trends, suggesting emerging problem areas that may need increased resources or intervention. By contrast, Parking Enforcement and Potholes are steady, suggesting these are ongoing, baseline issues rather than rapidly increasing problems. This visualization helps identify which service areas have growing demand over time and where city response efforts may need to adapt to shifting patterns in resident needs.

Our Process

We began by cleaning the original dataset in R Studio, removing duplicates and incomplete service requests to ensure data quality. For visualization, we built this interactive webpage using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Tableau was used to create dynamic dashboards, while Leaflet enabled us to map service requests geographically across Seattle. Additionally, we leveraged Plotly to develop 3D visualizations, highlighting trends by department and council district.
